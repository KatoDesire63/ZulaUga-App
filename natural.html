Natural Attractions

Experience the stunning beauty of our natural attractions.

Mountains

Rwenzori Mountains

Also known as the "Mountains of the Moon",the Rwenzori Mountains are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. They are renowed for their stunning alpine landscapes, glaciers,and diverse ecosystems. The range is part of the Rwenzori Mountains National Park.

    Key Peaks
  • Margherita Peak(5,109 meters
  • Mount Speke(4,890 meters)
  • Mount Baker(4,844 meters)

Location:Western Uganda, along the border with the Democratic Republic of Congo.

Mount Elgon

An extinct shield volcano with one of the largest calderas in the world. It is rich in biodiversity and home to Mount Elgon National Park.

Key Peak: Wagagai(4,321 meters)

Location: Eastern Uganda,on the border with Kenya.

Virunga Mountains

A chain of volcanic mountains famous for its population of mountain gorillas, within Mgahinga Gorilla National Park.

    Key Peaks
  • Mount Muhavura(4,127 meters)
  • Mount Mgahinga(3,474 meters)
  • Mount Sabinyo(3,669 meters)

Location: Southwestern Uganda, along the borders with Rwanda and the Democratic Republic of Congo.

Mount Moroto

Part of the Eastern Rift Valley, offering rugged terrain and significant to the local Tepeth community.

Key Peak: Mount Moroto(3,083 meters).

Location: Northeastern Uganda, near the border with Kenya.

Mount Zulia

Less prominent in height but significant within the local geography.

Location: Northwestern Uganda,near the border with South Sudan.

Beaches

Lutembe Beach

Its known for its tranquil environment and beautiful views of Lake Victoria. It's a great spot for birdwatching, especially flamingos,and offers ascrene escape with its sandy shores and gentle waves.

Location:Entebbe.

Imperial Resort Beach

Part of the Imperial Resort Beach Hotel, this beach is well-maintained and offerd various amenities,including restaurants,bars,and water sports.The beach is clean and family-friendly ,making it a popular choice for both locals and tourists.

Location:Entebbe.

Lido Beach

One of Uganda's oldest beaches,Lido Beach is known for its lively atmosphere.It features beach bars, music, and weekend events, making it a hotspot for social gatherings.The sandy beach and warm waters of Lake Victoria make it an inviting destination for swimming and relaxation.

Location:Entebbe.

One Love Beach

Owned by the famous Uganda musician Bobi Wine, One Love Beach is a vibrant spot that hosts numerous music concerts and cultural events. The beach offers a laid-backatmosphere with stunnung views of Lake Victoria,making it a popular spot for both relaxation and entertainment.

Location:Buabala,near Kampala.

Waterfalls

Source of the Nile

The source of the Nile is located at the point where the river emerges from Lake Victoria in Jinja. This is considered the starting point of the Nile River, which is one of the longest rivers in the world. The area around the source is a popular tourist destination, offering activities such as boat cruises,fishing,and visits to the nearby monuments. The source was famously confirmed by the explorer John Hanning Speke in 1862. The surroundings are lush and green,providing picturesque views of the river and lake.

Location:Jinja,Eastern Uganda.

Murchison Falls

Murchison Falls,also known as Kabalega Falls,are located on the Victoria Nile within Murchison Falls National Park. The falls are formed where the Nile forces its way through a narrow gorge,just 7 meters wide,before plunging 43 meters. The powerful flow creates a dramatic and thunderous display, making Murchison Falls one of the most impressive natural sights in Uganda.

Location:

Sezibwa Falls

Often overshadowed by its better-known namesake, the Ssezibwa Falls near Mukono are still relatively undiscovered.These falls are significant in Baganda culture and offer a scenic spot for picnicking, bird watching,and experiencing local traditions. The falls cascade over rock formations,creating picturesque pools and a screne environment.

Location:Near Mukono, Central Uganda.

Sipi Falls

Besides the main Sipi Falls,the area features two additional lesser-known waterfalls that contribute to th beauty of the Sipi River. Each fall has its unique charm,cascading over cliffs surrounded by green hills and coffee plantations. The area offers excellent hiking trails and breathtaking views of the Karamoja Plains.

Location:Near Kapchorwa, Eastern,Uganda.

Karuma Falls

Karuma falls are located on the Victoria Nile. The falls are characterized by their white-water rapids and are an important site for hydroelectric power generation.

Location:Near the town of Karuma, Northern Uganda.

Aruu Falls

Aruu Falls cascade down a series of rocks creating multiple small waterfalls and pools, making it a beautiful and tranquil location. The falls are surrounded by verdant vegetation and offer a refreshing spot for visitors to relax and swim. The area is relatively remote, providing a peaceful retreat away from more crowded toirist spots.

Location:Pader district, Northern Uganda.

Itanda Falls

Itanda Falls are powerful rapids in the Victoria Nile, downstream from the source of the Nile. Although not a traditional vertical waterfall,the rapids provide spectacular views and are a hotspot for white-water rafting enthusiasts.The surrounding scenery includes lush greenery and the roaring sound of the rapids,making it a thrilling experience for visitors.

Location:Near Jinja, Eastern Uganda.

Kisiizi Falls

Kissizi Falls is a picturesque waterfall with a tragic history,as it was once used as a punishment site for women who bore children out of wedlock. Today,the falls are a symbol of hope and are associated with a hospital that provides healthcare to local community. The waterfall is surrounded by cliffs and lush vegetation.making it a serene and historically significant location.

Location:Rukungiri district, Southwestern Uganda.

Parks

Bwindi Impenetable National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Bwindi is renowned for its population of mountain gorillas.The park features dense,mist-covered rainforest and is home to a rich diversity of flora and fauna,including over 120 mammal species,350 bird species,and many butterflies.

Location:Southwestern Uganda, on the edge of the Albertine Rift.

Queen Elizabeth National Park

Famous for its diverse ecosystems including savannah,humid forests,lakes,and fertile wetlands.The park is known for its tree-climbing lions in the Ishasha sector,large elephant herds, and the Kazinga Channel,which is teeming with hippos and crocodiles.

Location:Northwestern Uganda, stretching from Lake Albert to the northern part of the Albertine Rift Valley.

Kidepo Valley National Park

Known for its rugged savannah landscape and a high density of wildlife,Kidepo is one of Africa's last great wilderness areas.It features unique wildlife such as cheetahs,ostriches,and the Karamojong people who live nearby.

Location:Northeastern Uganda, bordering South Sudan and Kenya.

Kibale National Park

Kibale is famous for its primate population, particularly chimpanzees.The park's varied habitat supports one of the highest primate densities in the world,including red colobus monkeys and L'Hoest's monkeys.

Location:Western Uganda, near the town of Fort Portal.

Lake Mburo National Park

Known for its picturesque landscape,the park features rolling hills,acacia woodlands,and Lake Mburo itself.It is home to impalas, zebras,buffalos,and over 300 bird species.

Location:Western Uganda, near Mbarara town.

Uganda WildLife Education Center(UWEC)

Often referred to as Entebbe Zoo,is not just a zoo but also a conservation and education center. It houses rescued animals including lions,rhinos, chimpanzees,and numerous bird species.UWEC aims to educate the public about wildlife conservation and rehabilitate injured or orphaned animals.

Location:

Lakes

Lake Victoria

      The largest lake in Africa and the world's second-largest freshwater lake by surface area.
      Covers an area of about 68,800 square kilometers.
      Major towns on its shores include Entebbe,Kampala and Jinja.
      Source of the White Nile River.
      Supports a rich biodiversity,including numerous fish species like the Nile perch and tilapia.
      Key for fishing, transportation, and tourism.

Location:Southeastern Uganda, bordering Kenya and Tanzania.

Lake Albert

      One of the African Great Lakes,named after Prince Albert,consort of Queen Victoria.
      Appproximately 5,300 square kilometers in area.
      Part of the Albertine Rift,the western branch of the East African Rift.
      Drains into the Nile River through the Albert Nile.
      Important for oil exploration activities and local fishing communities.

Location:Western Uganda, on the border with the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

Lake Edward

      Another of the African Great Lakes, named after King Edward VII.
      Covers about 2,325 square kilometers.
      Connected to Lake George by the Kazinga Channel.
      Lies within Queen Elizabeth National Park, providing habitats for diverse wildlife.
      Supports local fisheries and wildlife tourism.

Location:Southwest Uganda, on the border with the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

Lake George

      Relatively small,covering around 250 square kilometers.
      Connected to Lake Edward by the Kazinga Channel.
      Lies within Queen Elizabeth National Park,contributing to the park's rich biodiversity.
      Known for its high productivity and diverse fish species.

Location:Central Uganda, east of Lake Victoria.

Lake Kyoga

      Covers approximately 1,720 square kilometers.
      Shallow and swampy,with an average depth of about 4 meters.
      Receives water from the Victoria Nile and drains into the Kyoga Nile.
      Important for local agriculture,fishing, and transport.

Location:Central Uganda, east of Lake Victoria.

Lake Bunyonyi

      Known as one of the deepest lakes in Africa, with depths reaching up to 900 meters.
      Covers an area of about 46 square kilometers.
      Famous for its scenic beauty,with numerous islands and terraced hills.
      Popular for eco-tourism,bird watching,and cultural experiences.

Location:Southwestern Uganda, near the Rwandan border.

Lake Wamala

      Covers approximately 250 square kilometers, although it fluctuates seasonally.
      A shallow freshwater lake with swampy shores.
      Important for local fishing and agriculture.

Location:Central Uganda, northwest of Kampala.

Forests

Mabira Forest

One of Uganda's largest surviving natural forests, Mabira is a moist semi-deciduous forest. It is home to over 300 bird species,various primates,and diverse plant life.

Location:Central Uganda, between Kampala and Jinja.

Budongo Forest

Part of the Murchison Falls Conservation Area, Budongo is a large tropical forest reserve known for its mahogany trees and significant chimpanzee population.The forest also supports a wide variety of bird species.

Location:Northwestern Uganda, near the town of Masindi.

Mgahinga Gorilla National Park

Part of the Virunga Volcanoes region,this park protects the Ugandan portion of the Virunga Mountains. It is a critical habitat for the mountain gorillas and golden monkeys.

Location:Southwestern Uganda, adjacent to Rwanda and the Democratic Republic of Congo.
